3 days Gorilla trekking in Bwindi impenetrable forest

Bwindi impenetrable national park is located in southwestern Uganda on the edge of the rift valley. It is a biologically diverse rainforest with about 450 mountain gorillas which is almost half the world’s population and almost 400 species of plants. The forest provides shelter to other animals including several other primate species like chimpanzees and baboons and over 350 species of birds.
Park activities· Gorilla habituation· Gorilla tracking · Nature walks· Tour of Batwa pygmy village.

3-day Murchison falls safari

Murchison falls National Park was established as one of Uganda’s first national park in 1952. Murchison falls which was also known as kabalega falls is a waterfall between lake albert and lake Kyoga on the Victoria Nile in Uganda. Murchison falls national park provides Uganda’s most remarkable wildlife including giraffes, elephants, buffaloes, hippos, Nile crocodiles and aquatic birds.
Park activities· Game drive· Boat cruise· Nature walks· Bird watching· Hiking to the top of falls· Sport fishing · Hot air ballooning safari

4-day Kidepo valley wildlife safari

Kidepo valley national park lies the semi-arid valleys between the boarder of Uganda and South Sudan in the north west just 5km from the eastern boarder of Kenya. The park contains two rivers, narus which disappears during the dry season leaving just pools for the animals and river kidepo. The park is surrounded by Karimojong pastoralists who are similar to the Masai of Kenya. The park is an important birding safari in Uganda with about 450 bird species.
Park activities· Nature walks/ hiking· Bird watching· Game drives· Cultural encounters
Park accommodation· Kidepo savannah lodge· Adere safari lodge· Nga’ moru wilderness camp· Apoka safari lodge

8-day Mt. Rwenzori hiking safari

Rwenzori mountain also known as mountain of the moon is a mountain range located in eastern Uganda on the boarder of Uganda and the Democratic republic of Congo. The highest peak of mt Rwenzori is Margherita at 5,109 meters (16,762 ft) which is the highest point in Uganda and third highest in Africa. Hiking to the summit of margherita peak is a lifetime experience with views of breath-taking scenery, a diversity of flora, streams of water and lakes and various species of birds and animals and finally the snow-capped glaciated mountain peaks.
